Admissions Guide

Everything you need to understand the university application process.

📅

The Academic Calendar

  • Study Year: Typically runs from September to May, though Australian and some Asian universities start in February or March.
  • Semesters vs. Quarters: A semester system divides the year into two halves (Fall and Spring). A quarter/trimester system divides the year into three or four shorter, faster-paced terms.
  • Credit Hours: The measurement of academic time. You usually need around 120 credits to graduate with a Bachelor's degree.
💰

Tuition & Financials

  • Tuition Fees: The base cost of your classes. International students usually pay significantly higher tuition than domestic citizens.
  • Room & Board: The cost of living in dormitories and eating at university dining halls. This is often an additional $10k–$20k per year.
  • Scholarships: Merit-based (awarded for high grades/achievements) or Need-based (awarded based on your family's financial income).
✍️

The Application Package

  • Motivational Letter (Personal Statement): An essay written by you. It explains who you are, why you want to study this major, and what makes you unique beyond your grades.
  • Recommendation Letters: Confidential letters written by your teachers or counselors. They vouch for your work ethic, character, and academic potential.
  • Transcripts: Your official high school report card showing your GPA (Grade Point Average) or IB/A-Level predicted scores.
🏆

Rankings & Statistics

  • QS World Ranking: A globally recognized ranking system based on academic reputation, employer reputation, and research citations.
  • Acceptance Rate: The percentage of applicants who get accepted. E.g., a 5% rate means only 5 out of 100 students are admitted (highly competitive).
  • "Fit" over Rank: While rankings are useful, finding a university with the right location, culture, and specific program is far more important for your success.
