Admissions Guide

Everything you need to master the global university application process.

The Academic Calendar

  • Study Year Typically runs from September to May (Fall and Spring), though universities in Australia and some parts of Asia start their academic year in February.
  • Semesters vs. Quarters A semester system divides the year into two halves. A quarter system divides the year into three shorter, faster-paced 10-week terms.
  • Credit Hours The measurement of academic workload. You usually need around 120 credits to graduate with a standard Bachelor's degree.

Tuition & Financials

  • Tuition Fees The base cost of taking classes. International students often pay significantly higher tuition rates compared to domestic citizens.
  • Room & Board The cost of living in campus dormitories and eating at university dining halls. This usually adds $10,000–$20,000 per year to your total bill.
  • Financial Aid Merit-based scholarships are awarded for high grades. Need-based aid is awarded depending on your family's financial income.

The Application Package

  • Motivational Letter (Personal Statement) An essay written by you. It explains who you are, why you chose your major, and what makes you unique beyond your grades.
  • Recommendation Letters Confidential letters written by your high school teachers. They vouch for your work ethic, intellectual curiosity, and character.
  • Transcripts & Tests Your official high school report card showing your GPA or IB/A-Level predicted scores, alongside SAT/ACT or IELTS/TOEFL test scores.

Rankings & Statistics

  • QS World Ranking A globally recognized ranking system based on academic reputation, employer reputation, and university research output.
  • Acceptance Rate The percentage of applicants who get accepted. For example, a 5% rate means only 5 out of 100 students are admitted (highly competitive).
  • "Fit" over Rank While rankings are a useful metric, finding a university with the right location, culture, and specific program is far more critical for your long-term success.
